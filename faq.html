Viasat Browser FAQs

You’ve come to the right place to learn more about the Viasat Browser web browser.

Viasat Browser general info:

What is Viasat Browser?

Viasat Browser is a new type of web browser that is faster than all leading browsers on Exede Internet. Plus, it is the first crowdsourced browser that learns over time. In other words, the more you use it, the better it gets. It also includes built-in ad-blocking software which makes it more secure than other leading browsers, while also helping to protect your privacy by blocking internet trackers. Currently it is only available for PC and Mac desktops.

How does Viasat Browser work?

Viasat Browser was built to find a smarter way to browse the internet. To this end, we rethought how browsers work and built an innovative hinting systems that allow Viasat Browser to study and learn webpages in order load the internet in a better, faster way.

Why should I install Viasat Browser?

By using Viasat Browser, you will have both a faster and safer web experience on Exede Internet when compared to leading web browsers. Plus, as a member of the Viasat Browser community, you will be helping to make a better web experience for all other users, just by surfing and enjoying the web like you always have, just faster.

How does it compare to other browsers?

Viasat Browser's features align competitively with leading browsers, but with additional speed and security. Thanks to its innovative hinting technology, it is able to load webpages faster the other leading browser on Exede Internet. See for yourself here. In addition, it has an integrated ad-blocker that help protect you by blocking most malware and internet trackers. In addition, get the content you love without unnecessary clutter or extra data consumption.

How are Viasat & Exede Internet connected?

Viasat is the owner of Exede Internet.

What platforms are Viasat Browser available for?

Currently, Viasat Browser is only available for Mac and PC computers. We will be expanding to mobile platforms in the future.

Viasat Browser beta program:

Why should I join the Beta?

You should join the Viasat Browser Beta because it gives you early access to our innovative new web browser which offers the fastest surfing on Exede Internet. Also, the more people using Viasat Browser, the faster it gets for everyone, helping all Viasat Browser users in addition to yourself. What do I need to be doing as a beta user?

The most important thing to do is to use the Viasat Browser browser, because by simply surfing the web, you are making the browser faster for everyone! We also appreciate feedback, so use the contact information on the page that opens when you start a new tab or browsing session to leave our developers a note and give updates about your experiences. If something goes wrong, you can also report the issue directly to the development team by right clicking on the sparrow icon near your address bar, selecting “Report a Sparrow Issue” and filling out the form.

I am having trouble with my invitation access code. What can I do?

If your code isn’t working as expected, try this: open the email on your PC or Mac and click the download link, then enter the code that was in your email and click the free download button. If that doesn't work or if you misplaced your code and would like another one, please email us at ProjectSparrow@viasat.com and we will get back to you with a fix or updated code.

What do I do if Viasat Browser doesn't seem faster?

During the beta, Viasat Browser randomly turns off some optimizations to place the browser in a Chromium-like mode in order to test Chromium performance versus Viasat Browser performance. To check if your browser is running in Chromium-like mode, go to sparrow://version and check if the word Chromiumlike appears in the command line. If it does, you can restore Sparrow optimizations by restarting your browser.

Getting started with Viasat Browser:

How do I make Viasat Browser my default browser?

Open the browser, click on the three-dotted menu in the top right hand corner, select "Settings", then scroll to the bottom and click the button that says "Make Sparrow the default browser."

How do I import my bookmarks?

Open browser, click on the three-dotted menu in the top right hand corner, select "Bookmarks", and then click "Import Bookmarks and Settings." From here you can choose from what browser you would like to import history, bookmarks, passwords and search engines.

Why does Viasat Browser look like Google Chrome?

Like many users, we love Chrome; we just wanted it better and faster. We built Viasat Browser using the same open-source code base, preserving the security features, useful extensions, and look and feel of Chrome, but adding our own innovations to make it better.

uBlock Origin:

What is uBlock Origin?

uBlock Origin is a free extension (appearing as the red shield in the right hand corner of the browser) we built into Viasat Browser to keep annoying ads from cluttering web pages and ruining you browsing experience. uBlock also protects you from potential Malware hidden in malicious ads and helps secure you privacy by blocking internet trackers. In addition, this helps reduce your data consumption by not loading unnecessary and unwanted content.

Some sites are asking me to turn off my ad blocker, what should I do?

You can exempt sites from uBlock Origin by clicking on the red shield icon next to your address bar and then clicking the blue power button so it becomes grey. This will allow ads to load on that particular site, but you will continue to get uBlock Origin’s protection on other sites.

Why can’t I reach a site with Viasat Browser but I can with another browser?

uBlock Origin, the built in ad blocker might be the issue, so try disabling it for that page. If that doesn’t work, try clearing your browser cache and cookies "from the beginning of time" by clicking on the three-dotted menu in the right hand corner, navigating to history, and clicking the "Clear browsing data" button.

Viasat Browser and other programs:

My antivirus is trying to block Viasat Browser, what should I do?

Because Viasat Browser is in beta, some antivirus programs may not recognize it and may try to block it. This can happen with many legitimate programs and can be easily rectified. If it happen here, you will need to unblock the Sparrow browser program, which is typically found in your antivirus's settings. If you aren’t sure how to do this, try Googling your antivirus’s name and "allow a blocked program."

My password manager doesn’t work with Viasat Browser, what can I do?

We are working with various password manager companies, but not all of them support Viasat Browser yet. Here are the ones that are currently confirmed to work:

  • LastPass
  • TrueKey
  • Dashlane (only on PCs)
  • 1Password (only on Macs)
